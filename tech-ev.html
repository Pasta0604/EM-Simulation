Lorentz Force Principle

EV motors function on the Lorentz force principle, where the interaction between an electric current and a magnetic field generates the torque necessary to turn the vehicle's wheels. This provides near-instantaneous power delivery and high efficiency across all RPM ranges.

Electromagnetic Induction

When an EV slows down, the electric motor is reversed to act as a generator. Using the principle of electromagnetic induction, the vehicle's kinetic energy is converted back into electrical energy and stored in the battery, significantly extending range.

Charging

Transformers & WPT

Electromagnetism is used in transformers to regulate voltage for wired charging and is the basis for wireless power transfer (WPT) systems that charge batteries via oscillating magnetic fields. High-voltage transformers manage the interface between grid distribution and vehicle storage.
